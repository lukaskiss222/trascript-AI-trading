American Express reported strong Q1 2019 earnings, with a 9% growth in FX-adjusted revenues and an EPS of $2.01, indicating solid financial performance. Management's tone was optimistic, emphasizing strategic partnership extensions, particularly the 11-year Delta Airlines deal, which positions the company for sustained growth. Despite facing FX headwinds, the company reaffirmed its 2019 guidance, expecting 8%-10% revenue growth. Overall, the positive momentum from new partnerships, robust customer acquisition, and cost-effective operations suggests a likely positive short-term impact on the stock.
