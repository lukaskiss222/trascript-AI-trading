American Express's Q2 2010 earnings call reflects solid financial performance with a 13% earnings increase and significant growth in billed business and average Cardmember spending. Despite a slight decline in managed total revenues, the company's strategic focus on charge and co-brand cards, which tend to involve higher spenders, bodes well, particularly with improving credit metrics and efficiencies in operations. On balance, the positive financial indicators and strategic direction suggest a neutral to slightly positive short-term impact on the stock price.
