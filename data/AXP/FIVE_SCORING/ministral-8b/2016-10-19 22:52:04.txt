The earnings call for American Express Company revealed a mixed bag of results. The company reported a 3% decrease in EPS for the third quarter, driven by lower revenues and higher investment spending. However, the company also reported strong growth in adjusted billings, loans, and revenue, as well as steady credit performance. Management highlighted the importance of cost reduction initiatives and the need to continue investing in growth opportunities, such as the Platinum card franchise and Small Business Saturday campaign. The company also announced plans to increase investment spending in the fourth quarter to support these initiatives. Overall, the earnings call provided a mixed signal to investors, with some positive developments but also concerns about the competitive environment and the potential impact of rising interest rates on the company's financial performance.
