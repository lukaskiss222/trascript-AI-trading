### Analysis

The earnings call for American Express Company in Q2 2019 revealed strong performance across various segments, with revenue growth at 10% on a FX adjusted basis and a solid increase in earnings per share (EPS). The company highlighted its focus on share, scale, and relevance, which has been driving consistent revenue and earnings growth. Key points include:

1. **Revenue Growth**: The company reported an 8% year-over-year revenue growth, with a strong performance in the consumer segment, particularly in the US and international markets.
2. **Credit Performance**: Credit performance remained strong, with loan growth at 11% and a 2.3% lending write-off rate.
3. **Operating Expenses**: Operating expenses grew at a rate slightly faster than revenue, driven by customer engagement costs and digital investments.
4. **Guidance**: The company reaffirmed its full-year guidance for revenue growth in the 8% to 10% range and adjusted EPS of $7.85 to $8.35.
5. **CECL Impact**: The company discussed its progress on CECL implementation and the potential impact on provision expenses.

Given the company's strong performance, consistent revenue growth, and positive outlook, the short-term impact on the stock price is likely to be positive.
