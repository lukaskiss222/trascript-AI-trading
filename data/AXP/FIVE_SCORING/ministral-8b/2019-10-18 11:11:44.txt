However, there were some concerns expressed about commercial spending trends and potential headwinds from macroeconomic factors. The company provided guidance for the fourth quarter and full year, expecting revenue growth of 8% to 10% and EPS between $7.85 and $8.35. The call also touched on the impact of CECL on provisions, with the company estimating a one-time increase in reserves of around 25% to 40%.
