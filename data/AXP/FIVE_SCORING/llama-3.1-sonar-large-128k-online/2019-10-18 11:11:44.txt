The Q3 2019 earnings call for American Express highlights several positive and stable trends that are likely to have a positive impact on the stock in the short term (1-2 weeks).

### Key Points:
- **Strong Revenue Growth**: American Express reported a 9% FX-adjusted revenue growth, driven by a well-balanced mix of spend, loans, and fees. This consistent revenue performance reinforces the company's ability to sustain high levels of growth.
- **Fee-Based Product Growth**: Net card fee growth accelerated to 19%, surpassing $1 billion for the first time, and 70% of new card members opted for fee-based products. This indicates strong customer engagement and a high-quality card base.
- **International Growth**: International consumer and SME segments showed strong growth, with FX-adjusted billings up 14% and 18%, respectively. This global growth momentum is expected to continue.
- **Credit Performance**: The company's credit trends remained stable and benign, with no significant change in the credit environment anticipated. This stability is crucial for investor confidence.
- **Guidance and Outlook**: The company reaffirmed its full-year adjusted EPS guidance of $7.85 to $8.35 and expects to maintain strong revenue growth in Q4, within the 8% to 10% range.

### Analysis:
The strong financial performance, positive trends in key segments, and reaffirmed guidance suggest that American Express is well-positioned for continued growth. The company's ability to maintain high revenue growth, coupled with its robust credit performance and strategic investments, should be viewed favorably by investors.

### Conclusion:
Given the strong Q3 results, the positive outlook, and the reaffirmed guidance, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term.
