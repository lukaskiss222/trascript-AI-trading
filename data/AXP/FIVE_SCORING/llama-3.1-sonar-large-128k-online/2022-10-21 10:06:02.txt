American Express reported strong Q3 2022 results, characterized by significant revenue growth, robust customer spending, and solid credit metrics. Here are the key points:

- **Revenue Growth**: Revenues grew 27% on an FX-adjusted basis, driven by strong spending in Goods & Services and a robust recovery in Travel & Entertainment (T&E) spending, which was up 57% year-over-year on an FX-adjusted basis[1][5|.
- **Customer Engagement**: The company saw high levels of customer engagement, with 3.3 million new proprietary card acquisitions, a record high. Millennial and Gen Z customers drove much of this growth, comprising over 60% of new card acquisitions[1][5|.
- **Credit Metrics**: Credit quality remained strong, with write-off rates and delinquency rates below pre-pandemic levels. The company built reserves but still maintains a strong credit profile[1][5|.
- **Guidance**: American Express reaffirmed its full-year revenue growth guidance of 23% to 25% and expects to be above its original EPS guidance range of $9.25 to $9.65[1][5|.
- **Macroeconomic Considerations**: Despite acknowledging macroeconomic uncertainties, the company expressed confidence in its growth plan and ability to adapt to changing economic conditions[1][5!.

Given these strong results, the positive guidance, and the robust spending trends, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term.
