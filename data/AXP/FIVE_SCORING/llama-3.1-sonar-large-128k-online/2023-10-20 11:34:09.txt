American Express reported strong Q3 2023 results, highlighted by several positive factors that suggest a favorable short-term outlook for the stock.

- **Record Revenues and EPS**: The company achieved its sixth consecutive quarter of record revenues, up 13% year-over-year to $15.4 billion, and EPS rose 34% to a record $3.30[3][5].
- **Strong Card Member Spending**: Card member spending remained robust, with a 7% year-over-year increase on an FX-adjusted basis, and significant growth in travel and entertainment spending[3][5].
- **Positive Demographic Trends**: Millennials and Gen Z consumers drove the highest billed business growth, accounting for over 60% of new consumer account acquisitions globally[3][5].
- **Enhanced Value Propositions**: The company continues to invest in innovating its products, such as the Resy platform and various card refreshes, which are attracting new premium customers and driving high levels of engagement and retention[3][5].
- **Best-in-Class Credit Performance**: Credit metrics remained strong, with delinquency and write-off rates below pre-pandemic levels[3][5].
- **Confident Guidance**: American Express raised its full-year revenue growth guidance and remains confident in achieving its long-term growth aspirations of over 10% revenue growth and mid-teens EPS growth[3][5].

Given these strong performance indicators, the company's ability to attract and retain premium customers, and the continued momentum in key revenue drivers, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term.
