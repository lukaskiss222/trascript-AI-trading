- **Customer Engagement**: Despite the decline in spending, American Express saw strong customer engagement, particularly in non-T&E categories, and maintained low attrition levels[6|.
- **Expense Management**: The company reduced marketing and operating expenses significantly, which helped offset some of the revenue decline[6|.
- **Liquidity and Capital**: American Express strengthened its capital and liquidity positions, providing a solid financial foundation to navigate the crisis[6|.
- **Long-term Strategy**: Management emphasized long-term strategies, including enhancing value propositions, expanding B2B payment solutions, and maintaining strong partnerships, which indicate a positive outlook beyond the current challenges[6!.

### Short-term Impact:
Given the immediate context of significant revenue declines, increased credit reserves, and ongoing economic uncertainty, the short-term impact on the stock is likely to be negative. Although the company's strong customer engagement, cost management, and financial stability are positive factors, they may not immediately offset the broader market concerns about the pandemic's impact.
