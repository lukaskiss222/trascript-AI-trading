1. **Adjusted Guidance**: 3M adjusted its full-year EPS guidance downward to $6.60 to $6.85, primarily due to macroeconomic uncertainties, a stronger U.S. dollar, and challenges in the Electronics and Energy segment. This adjustment could lead to short-term market caution.

2. **Mixed Segment Performance**: While some segments like Healthcare and Consumer showed strong organic growth, others like Electronics and Energy faced significant challenges, including lower demand and inventory corrections. This mixed performance might lead to some investor skepticism.

3. **Currency and Economic Headwinds**: The stronger U.S. dollar and lower factory utilization in some businesses are expected to continue affecting sales and earnings in the second quarter. These headwinds could maintain pressure on the stock in the near term.

4. **Positive Long-Term Outlook**: Despite short-term challenges, 3M's management remains confident in its long-term strategy, emphasizing continued investments in R&D, emerging markets, and productivity improvements. This could support the stock over the longer term but might not immediately alleviate short-term concerns.

Given these factors, the stock is likely to experience some short-term pressure due to the adjusted guidance, ongoing segment challenges, and macroeconomic uncertainties, despite the positive long-term outlook.
