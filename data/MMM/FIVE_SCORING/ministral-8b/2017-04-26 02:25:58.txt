### Analysis

The earnings call for 3M Company in Q1 2017 revealed a strong start to the year with robust organic growth across most business groups. The company reported 5% organic growth, driven by strong performance in Electronics & Energy, Industrial, and Safety & Graphics. The Consumer business, however, faced challenges due to channel adjustments in the office market.

Management highlighted their strategic investments, which included $136 million in the first quarter, with a significant portion allocated to growth initiatives. The company also mentioned plans for further strategic investments throughout the year, with expectations of growth contributions from these investments in the range of 50 to 100 basis points.

The guidance for 2017 was revised upward, with the company expecting organic growth of 2% to 5% and earnings per share of $8.70 to $9.05. This revision reflects the company's confidence in its growth prospects and the positive impact of strategic investments.

### Conclusion

The earnings call for 3M Company in Q1 2017 was largely positive, with strong organic growth and increased earnings per share. The company's strategic investments and revised guidance indicate a bullish outlook for the remainder of the year. However, the challenges faced by the Consumer business and the potential impact of geopolitical risks on the company's global operations could pose some risks.

Overall, the short-term impact on the stock price is likely to be positive, given the strong financial performance and upward revision of guidance. The stock is expected to rally in the next 1-2 weeks.
