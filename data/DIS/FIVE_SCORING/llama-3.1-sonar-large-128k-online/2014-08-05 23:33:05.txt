The Walt Disney Company's Q3 2014 earnings call highlighted several positive factors that are likely to have a short-term positive impact on the stock price. Here are the key points:

- **Record Earnings**: The company reported its highest quarterly earnings per share (EPS) of $1.28, exceeding previous records and indicating strong financial performance across various segments[2][4][5].
- **Strong Franchise Performance**: Successful franchises like Marvel's "Guardians of the Galaxy," Disney Animation's "Frozen," and the upcoming "Star Wars" series are driving significant growth and are expected to continue doing so[5].
- **Parks and Resorts Growth**: Domestic parks saw an 8% increase in revenue and a 23% increase in operating income, with positive trends expected to continue due to initiatives like MyMagic+[5].
- **ESPN and Broadcasting**: Despite higher programming costs, ESPN had a strong upfront and saw increased ad revenue, with the new SEC Network launch expected to be highly successful[5].
- **Consumer Products and Interactive Media**: Both segments showed robust growth, with consumer products delivering double-digit growth and Disney Interactive reporting its fourth consecutive quarter of profitability[5].

Given these strong results and the positive outlook across multiple business segments, the stock is likely to experience a short-term rally.
