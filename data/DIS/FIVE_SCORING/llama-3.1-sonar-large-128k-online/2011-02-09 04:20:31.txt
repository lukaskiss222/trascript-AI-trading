The Walt Disney Company's Q1 2011 earnings call presents several positive indicators that are likely to have a favorable short-term impact on the stock price. Here are the key points:

- **Strong Financial Performance**: Disney reported a 54% increase in net income to $1.3 billion and a 10% revenue increase, with EPS growing 45% to $0.68. This robust financial performance is a significant positive factor[2][3].
- **Segment Performance**: All major segments, including Media Networks, Parks and Resorts, Studio Entertainment, and Consumer Products, showed strong growth. Media Networks saw a 47% increase in profits, and Parks and Resorts delivered double-digit operating income growth despite challenging weather conditions[2][3].
- **Positive Trends**: The company experienced growth in advertising revenue, higher guest spending at parks, and strong performance from recent film releases like "Tangled" and "Toy Story 3". The launch of the Disney Dream cruise ship also boosted bookings across the entire fleet[2][3].
- **Guidance and Outlook**: While there are some comparability issues and tough comps ahead, the overall tone from management remains optimistic, especially regarding future growth opportunities and the company's strategic initiatives[2][3].

Given these factors, the stock is likely to see a positive impact in the short term.
