The Walt Disney Company's Q2 FY '15 earnings call highlighted several positive factors that are likely to have a short-term positive impact on the stock price. Here are the key points:

- **Strong Financial Performance**: Disney exceeded analyst expectations with adjusted EPS of $1.23, a 11% increase over the prior year, driven by revenue growth of 7% to $12.5 billion[1][3][4].
- **Successful Movie Releases**: The phenomenal success of "Avengers: Age of Ultron" and the anticipated release of "Star Wars: The Force Awakens" contribute to high investor optimism[3].
- **Parks and Resorts Growth**: Operating income in this segment increased by 24%, driven by higher guest spending and attendance at domestic parks[1][3].
- **Consumer Products and Media Networks**: Significant growth in consumer products, particularly from "Frozen" and "Avengers" merchandise, and strong performance in media networks, including ESPN and ABC, further bolster investor confidence[1][3].
- **Future Outlook**: The upcoming opening of Shanghai Disney Resort, new movie releases, and strategic initiatives in consumer products and media networks suggest continued growth potential[1][3].

Given these strong results and the promising outlook, the stock is likely to see a positive impact in the short term.
