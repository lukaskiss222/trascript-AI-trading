

The Walt Disney Company's Q1 2014 earnings call revealed strong financial performance across all segments, with operating income up 27% and revenue growth of 9%. The company's studio, driven by the success of Frozen and Thor: The Dark World, saw a 75% increase in operating income. Disney's Media Networks delivered 20% growth in operating income, led by higher affiliate and advertising revenues at ESPN. The company's consumer products division also saw a 24% increase in operating income, driven by the success of Frozen and Disney Junior properties. Given the strong earnings and positive guidance, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term.
