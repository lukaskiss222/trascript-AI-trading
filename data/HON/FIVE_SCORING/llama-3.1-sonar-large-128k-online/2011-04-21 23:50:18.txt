Honeywell International Inc.'s first-quarter earnings transcript indicates a strong start to 2011, with several positive trends that are likely to have a favorable short-term impact on the stock price.

- **Strong Financial Performance**: Honeywell reported better-than-expected sales of $8.9 billion, up 15%, and EPS of $0.88, a 40% increase over the previous year. This performance was driven by organic growth, new products, and geographic expansion.
- **Margin Expansion**: Segment margins expanded by 120 basis points to 14.5%, reflecting good volume leverage and continued cost controls.
- **Updated Guidance**: The company raised its full-year guidance, expecting earnings in the range of $3.80 to $3.95 per share, a 27% to 32% increase over the prior year.
- **Growth in Key Segments**: Aerospace, Automation and Control Solutions, Transportation Systems, and Specialty Materials all showed significant growth, with particular strength in emerging markets like China and India.
- **Positive Outlook**: The company's long-cycle businesses, such as Process Solutions and UOP, are accelerating, and the overall outlook for the year remains strong despite minor supply chain disruptions from Japan.

Given these strong results and the positive outlook, the stock is likely to experience a short-term positive impact.
