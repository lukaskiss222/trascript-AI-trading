Honeywell International Inc.'s Q1 2020 earnings call revealed a strong start to the year, with better-than-expected performance in many end markets, yielding EPS higher than the initial guidance. The company reported $7.8 billion in sales, up 3% year-over-year, and generated $673 million in free cash flow, a 190% increase from the prior year. Management raised full-year guidance, expecting earnings in the range of $2.30 to $2.45 per share, excluding pension expense. The company's focus on cost controls, productivity, and investments in growth initiatives has positioned it well for the recovery. Given the strong Q1 performance and optimistic guidance, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term.
