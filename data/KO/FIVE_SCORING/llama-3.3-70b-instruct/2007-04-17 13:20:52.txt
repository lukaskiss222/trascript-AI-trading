The Coca-Cola Company's Q1 2007 earnings call revealed a strong quarter with 17% revenue growth and 6% worldwide unit case volume growth, driven by international expansion and innovation in products such as Coke Zero. Despite a weak performance in North America, management expressed confidence in their ability to drive growth and improve execution. The company's guidance for 2007 remains positive, with an expected operating income growth of 11% and a dividend increase of 10%. Overall, the call suggests that the company is on track to meet its long-term growth targets, driven by its global portfolio and strategic initiatives.
