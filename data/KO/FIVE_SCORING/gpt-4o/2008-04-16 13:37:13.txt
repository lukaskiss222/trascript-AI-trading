The Coca-Cola Company's Q1 2008 earnings call highlighted strong international growth, especially in emerging markets like China, India, and Russia, with continued double-digit performance. Despite challenges in North America due to a weakening economic environment, Coca-Cola increased its volume and value market share, with the success of brands like Coca-Cola Zero and Glaceau suggesting solid market strategies. Management's optimistic tone and confidence in sustaining growth above long-term targets, combined with a potential for currency benefits reinvestment, point toward a positive short-term outlook for the stock.
