The Coca-Cola Company's third quarter 2007 earnings call presents several positive indicators that suggest a strong short-term outlook for the stock.

### Key Points:
- **Strong Financial Performance**: The company reported a 19% increase in revenues, driven by a 6% increase in concentrate sales, a 1% benefit from price mix, and a 4% increase from currency. Operating income grew by 12%, and cash from operations increased by 18%.
- **Global Volume Growth**: The company achieved 6% global unit case growth, with international operations leading the way with an 8% increase. This marks the tenth consecutive quarter of at least 4% volume growth.
- **Productivity and Efficiency**: The company is making significant progress in its productivity initiatives, which are resulting in operating expense leverage and allowing for reinvestment in top-line growth drivers.
- **Innovation and Market Expansion**: Successful execution of the three-cola strategy, strong performance from still beverages, and the integration of Glacau are driving growth and market share gains.
- **Positive Outlook**: Despite some uncertainty in the U.S. economy, the company remains confident in its ability to restore growth in North America and maintains a positive global macroeconomic outlook.

### Conclusion:
Given these strong financial results, continued global volume growth, productivity gains, and positive market expansion, the stock is likely to see a short-term positive impact.
