The Coca-Cola Company's second-quarter 2011 earnings call presents a strong and positive outlook for the company. Here are the key points that suggest a favorable short-term impact on the stock price:

- **Strong Financial Performance**: The company reported solid growth in revenue, volume, and earnings per share, exceeding long-term growth targets. Comparable EPS grew by 10% for the quarter and 9% year-to-date[1").

- **Global Volume and Value Share Gains**: Coca-Cola achieved worldwide volume growth of 6% for both the quarter and year-to-date, with significant growth in emerging markets like China, India, and Latin America. This indicates robust execution and market share gains[1].

- **Strategic Investments and Integration**: The integration of Coca-Cola Refreshments (CCR) is proceeding as planned, enabling strong execution of marketing and commercial programs. The company is also investing heavily in growth markets and new packaging strategies, such as single-serve packages in China, which are expected to drive long-term growth[1].

- **Positive Price and Mix**: The company implemented price increases and saw positive price/mix, which helped offset higher commodity costs. They expect to capture between 3% and 4% incremental retail pricing in the second half of 2011, contributing to profitable growth[1].

- **Confident Outlook**: Management expressed confidence in the company's ability to deliver against long-term targets, with a strong focus on executing the 2020 Vision. The company is well-positioned to navigate mixed economic conditions and continues to invest in brand strength, distribution, and innovation[1].

Given these points, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term due to the company's strong financial performance, strategic investments, and confident outlook.
