Microsoft's Q2 fiscal 2010 earnings showcased robust consumer demand for Windows 7, driving record revenue and profits, with a strong initial retail and online sales performance. However, enterprise spending has yet to recover significantly, potentially impacting future revenue in Office and server products. The positive points include successful product launches, Bing's market share gains, and the anticipation of Azure and Natal's impact. However, longer enterprise agreement cycles and uncertainties around enterprise spending suggest a cautious outlook. The guidance points to continued consumer strength, benefiting from past momentum, balanced against enterprise hesitance.
