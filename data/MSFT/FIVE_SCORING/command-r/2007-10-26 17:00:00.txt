Microsoft’s robust Q1 results reflected strong consumer demand and successful product launches. Impressive revenue and earnings growth beat expectations, driven by diverse business segments. The company’s guidance for continued double-digit growth across key segments is promising. However, the online services business lags, and investors may reflect concern over the sustainability of these figures given the impending launch of Windows Server and SQL Server in February 2008. Short-term outlook is positive, but Beautiful Mind-esque scenarios of untenanted success may be wishful thinking.
