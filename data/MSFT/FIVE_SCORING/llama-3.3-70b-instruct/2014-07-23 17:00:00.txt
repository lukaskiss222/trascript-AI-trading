Microsoft's Q4 earnings call highlighted the company's strong execution, with significant momentum in cloud services, progress in consumer businesses, and continued cost discipline. The company's commercial cloud revenue grew 147% year-over-year, driven by Office 365 and Azure, with a $4.4 billion annual run rate. Management's tone was positive, with a focus on strategic investments in areas like artificial intelligence, machine learning, and cloud infrastructure. The guidance for FY15 was generally in line with expectations, with operating expense expected to be down year-over-year, excluding the addition of Nokia costs. Overall, the call suggested a positive outlook for Microsoft's future growth and profitability.
