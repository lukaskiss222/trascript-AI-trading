Microsoft's Q4 and full-year earnings call revealed strong financial results, with record revenue, operating income, and earnings per share. The company's Windows division, Server and Tools business, and Entertainment and Devices division all achieved double-digit growth, driven by strong sales execution and customer demand. Management expressed optimism about the company's product momentum, cost discipline, and growth opportunities in the cloud and emerging markets. The company's guidance for fiscal year 2011 suggests continued growth, with revenue expected to grow in line with PC shipments and the Entertainment and Devices division expected to grow in the mid-teens.
