Microsoft's Q2 FY10 earnings call revealed a strong quarter with record revenue and profits, driven by consumer demand for Windows 7 and PCs. The company's guidance for the remainder of FY10 is positive, with expected revenue growth in line with the PC market. Management's tone was optimistic, citing momentum in the market and a strong product pipeline, including the upcoming launches of Office 2010 and Natal. While enterprise spending remains soft, the company is well-positioned for future growth, with a focus on managing expenses and aligning resources to high-priority areas.
