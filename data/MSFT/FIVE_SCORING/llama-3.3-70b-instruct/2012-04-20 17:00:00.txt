Microsoft's Q3 earnings call revealed a strong financial performance, with revenue growth of 6% to a record $17.4 billion and operating income growth of 12%. The company's enterprise segment drove growth, with businesses continuing to invest in Microsoft's products and services, particularly in the Server & Tools and Business divisions. Management expressed enthusiasm about the upcoming product launches, including Windows 8 and Office 15, and the company's unique position in the hybrid cloud space. The guidance for Q4 and fiscal 2013 was generally positive, with expected revenue growth and operating expense discipline. Overall, the call suggested a positive outlook for Microsoft's stock in the short term.
