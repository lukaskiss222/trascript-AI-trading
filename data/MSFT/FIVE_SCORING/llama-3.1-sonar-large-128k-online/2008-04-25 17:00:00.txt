Microsoft's third-quarter earnings call for fiscal year 2008 presented several key points that indicate a strong performance and positive outlook, despite some challenges and uncertainties.

### Key Points:
- **Revenue and Earnings Growth**: Microsoft reported 14% revenue growth and 27% earnings per share growth, adjusted for legal charges and tax benefits, beating their guidance[1>.
- **Diversified Business Model**: The company's diversified business across geographies, product categories, and customer segments helped mitigate economic uncertainties, with strong performance in server and tools, online services, and entertainment and devices divisions[1].
- **Fourth Quarter Guidance**: Microsoft expects a strong finish to the fiscal year, with revenue guidance of $15.5 billion to $15.8 billion and earnings per share of $0.45 to $0.48 for the fourth quarter[1].
- **Fiscal Year 2009 Outlook**: Preliminary guidance for fiscal year 2009 includes revenue growth of 11% to 13%, operating income growth of 18% to 19%, and earnings per share growth of 14% to 15%[1].
- **Yahoo Acquisition**: The proposed acquisition of Yahoo remains a significant factor, with Microsoft emphasizing their disciplined approach and the potential for alternative strategies if the deal does not proceed[1].

### Short-term Impact on Stock Price:
Given the strong quarterly performance, robust guidance for the fourth quarter, and a positive preliminary outlook for fiscal year 2009, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact. The company's ability to deliver consistent earnings growth, its diversified business model, and its strategic investments in high-growth areas all contribute to this positive outlook.
