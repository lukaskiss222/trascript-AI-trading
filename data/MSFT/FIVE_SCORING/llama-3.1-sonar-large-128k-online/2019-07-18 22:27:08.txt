Microsoft's Q4 FY19 earnings call presented several positive indicators that are likely to have a favorable impact on the stock price in the short term (1-2 weeks).

## Key Positives:
- **Strong Revenue and Profit Growth**: Microsoft reported a 12% increase in revenue and a 20% increase in operating income, with net income up 49% on a GAAP basis and 21% on a non-GAAP basis[2][3].
- **Cloud Performance**: Commercial cloud revenue grew 39% year-over-year, driven by Azure's 64% growth, indicating robust demand for Microsoft's cloud services[2][3].
- **Segment Performance**: All major segments (Productivity and Business Processes, Intelligent Cloud, and More Personal Computing) showed significant growth, with Office 365, Dynamics 365, and LinkedIn contributing notably[2][3].
- **Guidance**: The company provided strong guidance for FY20, expecting double-digit revenue growth and continued momentum in commercial cloud and other key areas[2][3].
- **Customer Commitment**: The company highlighted large, multi-year commercial cloud agreements and growing customer commitment, with contracted not recognized revenue up 25% year-over-year[2][3].

## Management's Tone and Guidance:
- The tone from Satya Nadella and Amy Hood was optimistic, emphasizing innovation, customer commitment, and the company's ability to drive growth across various business segments.
- The guidance for FY20 suggests continued investment in high-growth areas like cloud, AI, and business applications, which is expected to drive double-digit operating income growth[2][3].

Given these factors, the stock is likely to experience positive short-term momentum due to the strong earnings performance, robust cloud growth, and favorable guidance.
