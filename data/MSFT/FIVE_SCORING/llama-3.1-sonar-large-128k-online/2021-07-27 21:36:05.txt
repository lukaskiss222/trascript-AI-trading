Microsoft's Q4 FY21 earnings transcript indicates strong performance and positive outlook, which is likely to have a favorable impact on the stock price in the short term. Here are the key points:

2. **Cloud Growth**: The commercial cloud revenue grew 36% year-over-year to $19.5 billion, driven by Azure's 51% growth. This robust cloud performance is a key driver of Microsoft's success and is expected to continue[1][2][3].

3. **Positive Guidance**: The company provided optimistic guidance for FY22, expecting healthy double-digit revenue and operating income growth, despite some headwinds related to accounting changes and COVID-19-related savings[1][3].

4. **Innovation and Market Position**: Microsoft highlighted significant innovation across its product stack, including Azure, Dynamics 365, LinkedIn, and Microsoft Teams, which are driving long-term growth and competitive differentiation[1].

Given these factors, the stock is likely to see positive momentum in the short term due to the company's strong financial performance, robust cloud growth, and positive outlook.
