### Analysis

The earnings call for Microsoft Corporation presented a strong performance with record revenues, operating income, and earnings per share for both the fourth quarter and the full year. The company highlighted significant growth across all its divisions, particularly in the Windows, Server and Tools, and Online Services businesses. The Windows 7 adoption and the launch of new products like Office 2010, SharePoint 2010, and the Xbox 360 Kinect sensor were key drivers of growth. The company also reported robust bookings and a healthy contracted not billed balance, indicating strong enterprise demand. The management team provided an optimistic outlook for the first quarter and full year 2011, expecting revenue growth to be in line with PC shipments and continued strong demand for their products.

### Conclusion

The earnings call demonstrated a strong performance and a positive outlook for the company. The record revenues and earnings per share, along with the optimistic guidance, suggest that the stock will likely see a positive impact in the short term. The management's confidence in their product pipeline and the strong demand for their products are likely to be well-received by the market.
