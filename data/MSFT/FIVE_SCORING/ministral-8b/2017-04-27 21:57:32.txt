### Analysis

The Microsoft Corporation's Q3 fiscal year 2017 earnings call revealed robust financial performance, with notable growth across several segments. The company reported $23.6 billion in revenue, up 7% in constant currency, driven by strong performance in Productivity and Business Processes and Intelligent Cloud segments. Key highlights include:

- **Office 365 Commercial** seats grew 35% year over year, with revenue up 45% in constant currency.
- **Azure** revenue increased 93% year over year, with premium revenue growing triple digits.
- **Dynamics 365** revenue grew 82% in constant currency.
- **Windows** business grew 5% in constant currency, with the Creators Update driving new forms of creation and expression.
- **Gaming** business grew 4% in constant currency.

Management provided a forward-looking outlook, projecting $7.2 billion to $7.4 billion in Intelligent Cloud revenue and $8.2 billion to $8.4 billion in Productivity and Business Processes revenue for the fourth quarter. They also emphasized the importance of digital transformation and the integration of their various cloud services.

### Short-term Implications

The earnings call highlighted several factors that could impact Microsoft's stock price in the short term:

1. **Strong Revenue Growth**: The company reported significant revenue growth across multiple segments, which should be positively received by investors.
2. **Cloud Expansion**: The strong performance of Azure and the potential for growth in the cloud segment could drive further investment in the stock.
3. **Digital Transformation**: The emphasis on digital transformation and the integration of various Microsoft services could signal long-term growth opportunities.
4. **Productivity and Business Processes**: The growth in Office 365 and Dynamics 365 indicates a strong demand for productivity and business process solutions.
5. **Gaming and Windows**: The growth in gaming and the continued adoption of Windows 10 could also be positive signals for investors.

### Conclusion

The earnings call provided a strong indication of Microsoft's continued growth and the potential for long-term success in various segments. While there were some areas of concern, such as the decline in Surface revenue and the impact of LinkedIn on operating income, the overall performance was robust. The forward-looking guidance was also positive, indicating continued growth in key segments. Given these factors, the short-term impact on Microsoft's stock price is likely to be positive.
