Procter & Gamble reported solid Q3 results, meeting expectations on the top line and exceeding them on operating profit, EPS, and cash flow, driven by strong cost savings and productivity improvements. Despite facing choppy market conditions and foreign exchange headwinds, the company maintained its full-year EPS guidance, raised the low end slightly, increased its dividend, and upped its share repurchase target. Management expressed confidence in their ongoing strategies and noted improving market share trends, suggesting a positive short-term outlook for the stock.
