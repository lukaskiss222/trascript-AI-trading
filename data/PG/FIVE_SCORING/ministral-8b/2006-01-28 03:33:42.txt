The earnings call for Procter & Gamble (P&G) in the second quarter of 2006 revealed a solid performance with strong organic sales growth and balanced top and bottom line results. Key points include:

2. **Strong Top Line Growth**: Total sales increased by 27%, with organic sales growth of 6%.
3. **Operating Margin**: Operating margin was up 60 basis points versus year ago.
4. **Gross Margin**: Gross margin remained flat at 52.4%, despite higher commodity costs.
5. **Pricing and Mix**: Price mix was up 2%, primarily due to pricing actions to recover higher commodity costs.
6. **Foreign Exchange**: Foreign exchange had a 2% reduction to the top line, mainly due to the strengthening dollar versus the Euro, pound, and yen.
7. **Gillette Integration**: The integration of Gillette is progressing smoothly, with minimal business disruption. Revenue and cost synergies are being realized.
8. **2006 Guidance**: For 2006, P&G expects organic sales to grow 6% to 7%, with an all-in sales growth of 18% to 20%. Earnings per share are expected to be in the range of $2.58 to $2.62.
