### Analysis:

The Procter & Gamble Company (P&G) reported its Q4 and full-year results, highlighting strong organic sales growth and balanced top and bottom line performance. Key highlights include:

- **Q4 Results:** P&G achieved 8% total sales growth, driven by 5% organic volume growth, with developing markets leading the way.
- **Guidance Update:** The company expects 4% to 6% organic sales growth for the fiscal year 2008, with all-in sales growth of 6% to 8%. EPS is expected to be in the range of $3.46 to $3.49, up 14% to 15% year-over-year.
- **Operating Margin:** Operating margins are expected to improve by 50 to 100 basis points, driven by lower overhead costs and modest gross margin improvement.
- **Tax Rate:** The tax rate is expected to be at or slightly below 29%, excluding a one-time tax benefit of $0.02 per share.
- **Share Repurchases:** P&G expects to repurchase $8 billion to $10 billion worth of shares for the fiscal year.

### Short-Term Impact:

1. **Positive News:**
   - Balanced top and bottom line growth.
   - Strong organic sales growth, particularly in developing markets.
   - Improved operating margins and gross margins.
   - Confidence in sustainable growth model.

2. **Negative Factors:**
   - Increased commodity and energy costs impacting gross margins.
   - Potential consumer slowdown in the U.S.
   - Speculation about divestitures and restructuring charges.
