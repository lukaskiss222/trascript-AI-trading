The Q1 2006 earnings call for Apple Inc. revealed a strong performance with revenue of $5.75 billion, a 65% year-over-year increase. The company's net income nearly doubled, driven by record iPod sales and solid Mac sales. The guidance for the March quarter projects $4.3 billion in revenue, a 33% year-over-year growth. Management highlighted the challenges of the Intel transition and the expectation of a seasonal decline in iPod sales. The stock may experience short-term volatility due to the transition and seasonality, but the long-term growth prospects remain positive.
