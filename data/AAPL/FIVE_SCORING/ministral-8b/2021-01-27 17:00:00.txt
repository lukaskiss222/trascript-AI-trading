### Analysis:

The earnings call for Apple Inc. revealed a robust performance for the December quarter, with record revenue of $111.4 billion and strong growth across all product categories. The company's success was driven by the iPhone 12 family, which saw enthusiastic customer response despite COVID-19 impacts. The iPhone 12 Pro and Pro Max models in particular were highly popular, leading to supply constraints during the quarter. The Services business also performed exceptionally well, with an all-time record of $15.8 billion in revenue.

Management highlighted the company's strong installed base of active devices and the continued growth in switchers and upgraders. The iPhone 12 family's success, coupled with the introduction of the Apple One bundle, contributed significantly to the quarter's performance. Despite the challenges posed by COVID-19, Apple's ability to innovate and execute has been a key factor in its success.

### Conclusion:

The short-term implications for Apple's stock are positive due to the company's strong financial performance and the continued growth in its product categories. The iPhone 12 family's success and the introduction of the Apple One bundle are likely to drive further growth in the coming quarters. However, the stock may face some short-term pressure due to the uncertainty around the COVID-19 pandemic and potential supply chain disruptions.
