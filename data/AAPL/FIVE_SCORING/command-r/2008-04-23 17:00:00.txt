Apple's strong earnings in Q2 2008 impressed the market. Their diverse product range, including Macs, iPods, and iPhones, drove significant growth. However, delayed revenue recognition for iPhones sold after the March 6th announcement of iPhone 2.0 software hurts short-term financials. The management's cautious approach to recognizing this revenue impacts the bottom line in the immediate future. Despite this, the stock is likely to maintain its strength, supported by Apple's innovative product ecosystem and robust pricing strategies.
