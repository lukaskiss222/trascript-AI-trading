Apple's Q1 2008 earnings report showed record revenue and earnings, driven by strong Mac, iPod, and iPhone sales. The company's guidance for the March quarter indicates a sequential decline in revenue, but still represents 29% year-over-year growth. Management expressed confidence in their product pipeline and strategy, and cited the successful launch of the iPod Touch and the MacBook Air as evidence of their ability to innovate and drive growth. Overall, the tone of the call was positive, with management emphasizing their focus on delivering innovative products and services that delight customers.
