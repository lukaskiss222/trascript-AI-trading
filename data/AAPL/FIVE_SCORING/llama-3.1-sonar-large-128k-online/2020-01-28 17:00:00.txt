Apple's Q1 FY2020 earnings call presented several positive indicators that are likely to have a favorable short-term impact on the company's stock price. Here are the key points:

## Strong Financial Performance
- Apple reported an all-time record revenue of $91.8 billion, a 9% increase year-over-year, and a 19% increase in earnings per share to $4.99, also an all-time record[1][2][4].

## Product and Services Growth
- iPhone revenue grew 8% year-over-year, driven by strong demand for the iPhone 11 series. Services revenue reached a new all-time record of $12.7 billion, growing 17% year-over-year[1][2][4].

## Expanding Ecosystem
- The active installed base of devices surpassed 1.5 billion, with significant growth in all geographic segments. Wearables, Home, and Accessories revenue set a new all-time record, up 37% year-over-year[1][2][4].

## Guidance and Outlook
- Despite the uncertainty related to the coronavirus outbreak, Apple provided a robust guidance for the March quarter, with revenue expected between $63 billion and $67 billion. The company also highlighted a favorable commodity pricing environment and cost savings that will help maintain good gross margins[1][2][4].

## Investor Returns
- Apple returned nearly $25 billion to shareholders during the quarter, including share repurchases and dividends, and declared a cash dividend of $0.77 per share[1][2][4].

Given these strong results, the positive tone from management, and the robust guidance, Apple's stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term.
