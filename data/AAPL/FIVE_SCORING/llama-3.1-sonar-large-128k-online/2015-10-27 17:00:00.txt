Apple's Q4 2015 earnings transcript indicates several positive and a few challenging aspects that could influence the stock price in the short term:

### Positive Factors
- **Strong Financial Performance**: Apple reported a record September quarter with $51.5 billion in revenue, a 22% year-over-year increase, and a net income of $11.1 billion, surpassing Wall Street expectations[1][2][3].
- **iPhone Sales**: iPhone sales were strong, with 48 million units sold, up 22% year-over-year. The new iPhone 6s and 6s Plus models saw high demand, and the company reported a significant number of Android switchers[1][2][3].
- **Services Growth**: The App Store and other services saw significant growth, with a 25% increase in App Store revenue and a 10% increase in overall services revenue[1][3].
- **Enterprise Growth**: Apple's enterprise business grew 40% year-over-year, reaching $25 billion in annual revenue, highlighting a strong and expanding segment[1][3].

### Challenging Factors
- **Foreign Exchange Headwinds**: The company faced significant foreign exchange headwinds, which impacted revenue and margins. However, they managed to mitigate some of this impact through hedging and price adjustments[1][3].
- **iPad Sales**: iPad sales declined 20% year-over-year, although the company remained optimistic about the segment's performance in specific markets[1][3].
- **Inventory and Demand**: While demand for new iPhones exceeded supply, the company expressed confidence in their manufacturing ramp and inventory management[1][3].

### Guidance and Outlook
- **Q1 2016 Guidance**: Apple provided guidance for Q1 2016, expecting revenue between $75.5 billion and $77.5 billion, with gross margins between 39% and 40%. Despite foreign exchange headwinds, the guidance was strong[1][3].

### Conclusion
Given the strong financial performance, positive trends in iPhone sales and services, and the company's ability to navigate foreign exchange challenges, the short-term outlook for Apple's stock is likely positive. The company's growth in emerging markets, enterprise segment, and innovative product lineup further support this optimism.
