Apple's Q2 FY 2021 earnings call revealed several key points that indicate a strong short-term outlook for the company's stock.

### Key Points:
- **Record Revenue and Earnings**: Apple reported a March quarter record revenue of $89.6 billion, up 54% year-over-year, and earnings per share of $1.40, significantly beating analyst estimates[2][3][4].
- **Strong Product Performance**: All product categories, including iPhone, Mac, iPad, and Wearables, Home and Accessories, saw significant year-over-year growth. iPhone revenue grew 66%, Mac revenue grew 70%, and iPad revenue grew 79%[3][4].
- **Services Growth**: Services revenue reached an all-time high of $16.9 billion, growing 27% year-over-year, driven by strong growth in Apple Care, advertising, and other services[2][3][4].
- **Guidance and Supply Constraints**: While Apple did not provide specific revenue guidance for the June quarter due to COVID-19 uncertainties, they indicated strong double-digit year-over-year revenue growth but noted potential supply constraints affecting iPad and Mac sales[3][4].
- **Dividend and Share Repurchases**: Apple announced a 7% increase in its dividend and an additional $90 billion for share repurchases, indicating confidence in the company's financial health[2][3][4].

### Short-Term Impact:
Given the robust performance across all product categories, the significant beat on earnings and revenue, and the positive guidance despite supply constraints, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term.

### Conclusion:
The strong financial results, record-breaking revenues, and the company's confident outlook on future growth, despite some supply chain challenges, suggest that the stock will see a positive reaction.
