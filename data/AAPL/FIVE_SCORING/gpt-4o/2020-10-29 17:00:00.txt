Apple Inc. demonstrated strong performance in the fourth quarter, setting a September quarter revenue record of $64.7 billion despite challenges such as iPhone release delays and COVID-19 impacts. CEO Tim Cook and CFO Luca Maestri expressed optimism due to strong demand across product categories, particularly for services, Macs, and iPads, with Apple achieving new records in several areas. The positive reception to new iPhone models and the promising outlook for 5G adoption contribute to an overall bullish sentiment. Given these factors, Apple's stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term as investor confidence is buoyed by the robust performance and future growth prospects.
