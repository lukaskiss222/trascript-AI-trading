Apple reported a strong performance for Q3 2010, showcasing record revenue driven by robust sales of the iPad, iPhone 4, and Macs. The company experienced higher-than-expected iPhone sales and accessory revenue, contributing positively to margins, although concerns were noted regarding potential supply constraints for the iPhone 4 and iPad. Nonetheless, Apple's projections for the next quarter were optimistic, with revenue expected to reach $18 billion despite anticipated costs associated with iPhone 4 case offers. Overall, the upbeat results and guidance, coupled with successful product launches, suggest a positive short-term impact on Apple's stock.
