Caterpillar's Q2 2020 earnings call revealed a significant sales decline, largely driven by lower end-user demand and inventory reductions by dealers.  While the decline was less severe than anticipated, management withdrew their outlook for the year and emphasized the ongoing uncertainty.  Caterpillar's  strong cash position and liquidity, their focus on cost-cutting measures, and continued investment in digital capabilities suggest the company intends to weather the economic storm and maintain a strong balance sheet.
