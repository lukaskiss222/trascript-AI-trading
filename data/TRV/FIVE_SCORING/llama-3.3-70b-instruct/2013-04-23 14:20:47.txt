The Travelers Companies, Inc. reported strong Q1 2013 results, with operating income of $2.31 per diluted share, representing the highest quarterly operating income per share since the company's initial public offering in 2002. The company's margins improved significantly, with a GAAP combined ratio of 88.5% and an underlying combined ratio of 90.8%, which was an improvement of almost 4 points from the prior year. Management's tone was positive, and they expressed confidence in their ability to continue improving accident year profitability and returns. The company's guidance for 2013 was also positive, with projected sales of $715M-$836M and EPS of $4.55-$5.05. Overall, the results and management's tone suggest a positive short-term outlook for the stock.
