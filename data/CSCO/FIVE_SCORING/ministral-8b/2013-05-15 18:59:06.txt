### Analysis:
The earnings call for Cisco Systems, Inc. indicates a strong performance in the third quarter of FY2013, with record revenue and earnings growth. Key points include:

1. **Record Revenue and Earnings Growth**: Cisco delivered its ninth consecutive quarter of record revenue, with a 5% increase in total revenue and a 6% increase in non-GAAP EPS.

2. **Product and Geographic Performance**: Strong growth in cloud data center (77%), wireless (27%), and SP Wi-Fi (100%) revenue, along with positive year-over-year order growth across all four customer segments (enterprise, commercial, service provider, and public sector).

3. **Operational Efficiency**: Cisco continued to improve its operational discipline and focus on gross margin stability. The company achieved a non-GAAP gross margin of 63.0% and a non-GAAP operating margin of 28.2%.

4. **Acquisitions and Strategic Investments**: Cisco completed the divestiture of Linksys and announced two more acquisitions in software and cloud, aligning with its strategy to drive growth and innovation.

5. **Cash and Dividend Policy**: Cisco generated $3.1 billion in operating cash flow and returned $1.8 billion to shareholders through share buybacks and dividends. The company announced a $0.17 per share quarterly dividend, an increase of $0.03 per share.

6. **Outlook**: Cisco provided guidance for the fourth quarter, expecting revenue growth of 4% to 7% and non-GAAP EPS of $0.50 to $0.52 per share.

### Short-Term Impact:
The earnings call presents a positive picture for Cisco, with strong revenue and earnings growth, improved operational efficiency, and strategic investments. The company's focus on cloud data center, wireless, and service provider markets, along with its ability to maintain gross margins, suggests a robust outlook. However, the company's guidance for the fourth quarter is relatively conservative, reflecting the challenging macroeconomic environment. This could lead to a neutral to slightly positive reaction in the stock price over the next 1-2 weeks, as investors digest the positive results and the cautious outlook.
