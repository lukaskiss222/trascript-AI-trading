### Analysis:

Cisco's Q3 2009 earnings call revealed a solid performance amidst challenging market conditions. The company reported a 17% year-over-year decrease in revenue, primarily driven by the global economic slowdown. However, Cisco's strategic focus on innovation, cost management, and market adjacencies has positioned it well for the future.

Key points highlighted during the call include:
- **Financial Performance**: Cisco generated $2 billion in cash and maintained strong balance sheet metrics.
- **Market Transition**: The company is navigating the market transition with a focus on collaboration, virtualization, and video networking.
- **Operational Efficiency**: Cisco has successfully reduced expenses and improved gross margins.
- **Strategic Growth**: The company is expanding into new market adjacencies, including virtual healthcare, safety and security, and smart communities.
- **Customer Relationships**: Cisco's relationships with its customers and channels have never been tighter, with customers recognizing the value of Cisco's innovation and execution.

### Conclusion:

The short-term impact on Cisco's stock is expected to be neutral to slightly positive. The company's strong financial performance, focus on innovation, and strategic positioning in new market adjacencies suggest a positive outlook. However, the uncertain global economic environment may create some volatility in the stock price.
