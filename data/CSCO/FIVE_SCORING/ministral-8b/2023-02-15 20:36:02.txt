### Analysis

Cisco Systems, Inc. reported strong Q2 and full-year results, with revenue up 7% and record non-GAAP EPS of $0.88. The company exceeded its guidance ranges and delivered solid ARR growth, sequential non-GAAP margin expansion, and record non-GAAP net income. Key highlights include:

- **Supply Chain Improvements:** The company has made significant progress in improving supply chain constraints, leading to increased product deliveries and reduced customer lead times.
- **Customer Demand:** The company experienced normal double-digit sequential growth in both enterprise and commercial markets, while public sector performed better than historical ranges.
- **Product Performance:** Strong growth was seen in secure agile networks, enterprise routing, wireless, Meraki, DUO, ThousandEyes, and other segments.
- **Software Revenue:** Software revenue grew 10%, with software subscription revenue up 15%.
- **Backlog and RPO:** Record backlog and RPO provided significant visibility into future performance, with product RPO increasing 7% and service RPO increasing 2%.
- **Gross Margin:** Total non-GAAP gross margin was 63.9%, down 160 basis points year-over-year but up 90 basis points sequentially.
- **Operating Margin:** Non-GAAP operating margin was 32.5%, down 180 basis points year-over-year but up 70 basis points sequentially.
- **Share Buybacks and Dividends:** The company returned $2.8 billion to shareholders during the quarter, including $1.6 billion for the quarterly cash dividend and $1.3 billion of share repurchases. The quarterly dividend was raised by $0.01 to $0.39 per share.

### Conclusion

The short-term impact on Cisco's stock price is likely to be positive due to the strong financial results and improved visibility into future performance. The company's progress in addressing supply chain issues and the stability of demand suggest a positive outlook for the coming quarters. The guidance for Q3 and full year 2023 further supports this optimism, with the company expecting revenue growth in the range of 11% to 13% and non-GAAP EPS in the range of $0.96 to $0.98.
