The earnings transcript for Cisco Systems Inc. for the third quarter of fiscal year 2008 presents a mixed but generally positive outlook. Here are the key points:

- **Revenue and Profitability**: Cisco reported a 10.4% year-over-year increase in total revenue to $9.8 billion, which was above the midpoint of their guidance. Non-GAAP net income increased by 9.4% year-over-year to $2.3 billion, and non-GAAP EPS increased by 12% to $0.38[1].

- **Geographic and Segment Performance**: The company saw strong growth in Asia-Pacific, particularly in India and China, and a significant turnaround in Japan. The US market was softer, but other regions showed resilience. Service provider orders were challenging in the US but strong in other regions[1].

- **Innovation and Leadership**: Cisco highlighted significant product innovations, such as the ASR 1000, Nexus 7000, and Nexus 5000, and emphasized their leadership in collaboration enabled by network Web 2.0 technologies. The company also stressed its unique business model innovations and market transition strategies[1].

- **Guidance and Outlook**: Despite economic challenges, Cisco maintained its long-term growth projections of 12% to 17% year-over-year. For Q4, revenue growth was guided to be in the range of 9% to 10% year-over-year. The company expressed confidence in navigating economic slowdowns and gaining market share during such periods[1].

Given these points, the short-term impact on Cisco's stock price is likely to be positive due to the following reasons:

- Strong financial performance despite economic challenges.
- Positive guidance and long-term growth prospects.
- Significant innovations and leadership in key product areas.
- Diversified geographic performance with strong growth in key regions.
