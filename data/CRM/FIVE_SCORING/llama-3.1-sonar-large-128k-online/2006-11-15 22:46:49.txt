The Salesforce Q3 FY2007 earnings transcript presents several positive indicators that are likely to have a favorable short-term impact on the stock price:

1. **Record Revenue and Growth**: Salesforce reported a 57% year-over-year revenue increase to $130 million, exceeding expectations. This strong revenue growth, coupled with a significant increase in net paying subscribers (61,000 in the quarter), indicates robust business momentum.

2. **Strong Customer and Partner Engagement**: The company highlighted major customer wins, including Cisco, Dun & Bradstreet, and TD Ameritrade, and announced significant partnerships with IBM and Deloitte. These alliances are expected to drive further growth and customer success.

3. **Innovative Platform and Solutions**: The success of the AppExchange and the introduction of the Apex platform are seen as key drivers of future growth. These innovations are expanding Salesforce's capabilities beyond CRM and into broader on-demand application services.

4. **Positive Guidance**: Salesforce provided strong guidance for Q4 FY2007 and FY2008, with expected revenues of $493-495 million for the full year FY2007 and $700-710 million for FY2008. This positive outlook suggests continued confidence in the company's growth trajectory.

5. **Financial Health**: The company reported solid cash flow and a strong balance sheet, with cash and cash equivalents totaling $371 million. This financial health supports the company's ability to invest in growth initiatives.

Given these factors, the stock is likely to see a positive impact in the short term.
