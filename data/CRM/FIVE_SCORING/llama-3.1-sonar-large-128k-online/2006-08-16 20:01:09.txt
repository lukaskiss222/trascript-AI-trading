Salesforce.com's Q2 fiscal year 2007 earnings call highlights several positive factors that are likely to have a short-term positive impact on the stock price. Here are the key points:

- **Strong Financial Performance**: The company reported excellent financial results, including a 64% year-over-year revenue increase to $118 million, exceeding expectations. Non-GAAP EPS was $0.06, above the high end of the outlook.
- **Customer Growth and Success**: Salesforce added a record 57,000 net new subscribers, reaching a total of over 500,000 subscribers. Significant customer wins and expansions, such as those with Bear Stearns, Mizuho Financial Services, and Hitachi, were highlighted.
- **Product and Platform Innovation**: The launch of new services like OEM Edition and Salesforce Partner Edition, along with the continued growth of the AppExchange platform, demonstrates the company's innovation and expansion into new markets.
- **Operational Excellence**: The company's data center performance and system availability were outstanding, with over 3.1 billion transactions processed during the quarter.
- **Guidance and Outlook**: Salesforce raised its full-year revenue and EPS guidance, indicating continued confidence in its growth trajectory.

Given these strong financial results, significant customer additions, innovative product launches, and positive guidance, the stock is likely to see a short-term positive impact.
