Based on the earnings call transcript, The Home Depot Inc. reported strong financial results for the second quarter of 2021, with record sales of $41.1 billion, an 8.1% increase from the same period last year. The company also reported positive comps in all 3 months of the quarter, with a total company comp of 4.5% and US comps of 3.4%. Gross margin was 33.2%, down from 34.0% in the same period last year, primarily due to lumber, transportation costs, and investments in the supply chain. Operating expenses as a percent of sales decreased to 17.1%, and operating margin improved to 16.1%. The company also reported strong demand for home improvement products, with a shift in consumer patterns towards weekday sales and a continued focus on larger projects. The company remains focused on executing its strategic investments and expanding its interconnected shopping experience. Management also discussed the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on the business and the company's ability to manage through the cost environment effectively. Overall, the company's performance in the second quarter was strong, and the company remains optimistic about its long-term prospects.
