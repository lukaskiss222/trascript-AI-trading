The Home Depot's Q1 earnings call revealed a strong quarterly performance, with sales increasing 4.3% and diluted earnings per share rising 43.3%. The company's same-store sales were positive 4.8%, driven by a solid performance in seasonal businesses and improvements in core repair and maintenance categories. Despite some challenges in big-ticket and pro business, management expressed optimism about the company's prospects, citing improved customer service, better assortment management, and a strong supply chain. The company raised its fiscal 2010 guidance, expecting sales to increase approximately 3.5% and earnings per share to rise approximately 21%. With a solid Q1 performance and positive guidance, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term.
