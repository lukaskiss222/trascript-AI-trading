1. **Challenging Market Conditions**: The company faced significant headwinds in 2006 due to the slowdown in the home improvement and housing markets, resulting in negative same-store sales and lower earnings per share. This trend is expected to continue into 2007, which may weigh on investor sentiment.

2. ** Retail Performance**: The retail segment experienced a 6.6% negative comp in the fourth quarter, and while there were some positive notes such as successes in appliances and holiday decor, the overall retail environment remains challenging. This could lead to short-term stock pressure.

3. **Supply Business Uncertainty**: The consideration of strategic alternatives for the Home Depot Supply business introduces uncertainty. While the business performed well in 2006, the decision to explore selling it could impact investor confidence and create short-term volatility.

4. **Investment and Cost Structure**: The company is investing heavily in retail reinvestment programs, which, although expected to pay off in the long term, may not immediately reflect in the numbers. This ongoing investment, coupled with higher operating expenses and debt, could also influence short-term stock performance.

5. **Guidance and Outlook**: The company's guidance for 2007, to be detailed at the upcoming Investor Conference, is anticipated to reflect continued challenges. This lack of immediate optimism for a significant turnaround in 2007 might keep the stock price under pressure in the short term.

Given these factors, the short-term outlook for The Home Depot's stock is likely to be negative due to the ongoing market challenges, uncertainties around the Supply business, and the need for continued investment in the retail segment.
