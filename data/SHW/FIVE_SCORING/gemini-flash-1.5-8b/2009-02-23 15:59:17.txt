Sherwin-Williams' Q1 earnings, while slightly below expectations due to the overall economic downturn, show resilience.  Management highlights cost-cutting measures, positive pricing strategies amidst raw material cost inflation, and ongoing efforts in China, suggesting the company is managing the challenging environment effectively.  Improved product mix and manufacturing efficiency also contributed positively.  The outlook for the coming quarters remains cautious though, with expected continued pressure on volumes.
