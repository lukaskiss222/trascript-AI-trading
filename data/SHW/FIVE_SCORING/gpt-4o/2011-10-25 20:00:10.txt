Sherwin-Williams reported strong Q3 2011 results with record sales and earnings per share, driven by price increases and volume growth across all segments. Despite facing raw material cost pressures, management expressed confidence in maintaining price pass-throughs and achieving expected margins. They projected continued sales growth for Q4 and reaffirmed full-year guidance, demonstrating resilience in challenging economic conditions. The positive momentum, strategic share gains, and effective cost management suggest an optimistic short-term impact on the stock.
