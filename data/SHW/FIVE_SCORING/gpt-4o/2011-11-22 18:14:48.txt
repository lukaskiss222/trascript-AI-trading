Sherwin-Williams reported strong financial performance with double-digit growth in sales and earnings for the quarter, and positive projections for 2012, despite headwinds such as high raw material costs and a non-cash impairment charge. The management's tone was optimistic, highlighting successful new business initiatives, geographic expansion, and operational efficiencies, which they believe will continue driving earnings growth. The company's guidance for double-digit earnings growth in 2012, based on new business and pricing actions, suggests a positive short-term outlook for the stock.
