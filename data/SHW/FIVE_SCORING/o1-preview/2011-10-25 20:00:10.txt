Sherwin-Williams reported record sales and earnings in Q3 2011, driven by strong volume growth and successful strategic investments, indicating market share gains. However, rising raw material costs, particularly titanium dioxide, have pressured gross margins and are expected to continue impacting results in the near term. Despite these challenges, management's reaffirmed guidance and confidence in closing the input cost gap suggest a cautiously positive outlook, likely leading to a modest uptick in the stock price.
