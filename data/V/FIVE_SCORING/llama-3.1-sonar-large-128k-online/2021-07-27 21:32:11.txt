The earnings transcript for Visa Inc. indicates several positive trends and strong performance metrics that are likely to have a positive impact on the stock price in the short term (1-2 weeks).

- **Strong Revenue and Volume Growth**: Net revenues rose 27%, with payments volume increasing 34% year-over-year, and processed transactions up 39% year-over-year. These metrics exceeded expectations and reflect a robust recovery from the pandemic.
- **Cross-Border and Travel Recovery**: Cross-border volumes, excluding intra-Europe, were at 82% of 2019 levels, with a significant improvement in travel-related spending. This trend is expected to continue, especially with more borders opening.
- **Debit and Credit Growth**: Both debit and credit spending showed strong growth, with debit benefiting from cash digitization and credit improving due to the return of affluent consumer spending in sectors like travel, entertainment, and restaurants.
- **Value-Added Services**: These services grew 28%, driven by increases in consulting, data services, and other areas. The company is also seeing growth in new flows, including Visa Direct and B2B Connect.
- **Acquisitions and Partnerships**: The acquisition of Tink and Currencycloud, along with new partnerships, particularly in fintech and cryptocurrency, are expected to enhance Visa's capabilities and drive future growth.
- **Guidance and Outlook**: The fourth quarter outlook is positive, with net revenue growth expected to be in line with the third quarter, and operating expenses growing in the mid-teens to support continued growth initiatives.

### Conclusion:
Given the strong performance across multiple segments, positive guidance, and the momentum in new growth areas, Visa's stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term.
