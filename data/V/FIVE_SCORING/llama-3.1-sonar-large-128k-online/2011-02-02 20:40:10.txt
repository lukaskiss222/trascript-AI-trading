Visa Inc.'s fiscal first quarter 2011 earnings call presented several positive and reassuring points that are likely to have a positive short-term impact on the stock price. Here are the key reasons:

1. **Strong Financial Performance**: Visa reported a 14% increase in net operating revenues to $2.2 billion and a 16% increase in net income to $884 million, driven by double-digit growth in payments volume, cross-border volume, and Visa-processed transactions[5].
2. **Global Growth**: The company saw significant growth in Rest of World markets, with 60% of total revenue growth coming from outside the United States. This aligns with their long-term goal of increasing international revenue[5].
3. **Innovation and Expansion**: Visa highlighted progress in key initiatives such as eCommerce, Money Transfer, and mobile payments, which are expected to drive future growth. The 25% year-over-year growth in eCommerce payments volume and the expansion of CyberSource are particularly noteworthy[5].
4. **Share Repurchase Program**: Visa actively executed a $1 billion share repurchase program, buying back over $300 million worth of shares in the quarter, which is a positive signal for shareholders[5].
5. **Confidence in Long-Term Growth**: Despite the uncertainties around the Durbin amendment, the management expressed confidence in Visa's ability to deliver strong revenue and earnings growth over the long term through diversified strategies and mitigation efforts[5].

Given these strong points, the stock is likely to see a positive impact in the short term.
