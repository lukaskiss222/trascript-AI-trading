Based on the earnings transcript, Visa Inc.'s second-quarter results were robust, with net revenues up 25% year-over-year and non-GAAP EPS up 30%. The company's performance was driven by strong cross-border travel recovery, accelerating debit card usage, and growth in value-added services. Despite the suspension of operations in Russia and the impact of Omicron, Visa's growth engines of consumer payments, new flows, and value-added services continued to perform well. The company expects to achieve a high-teens to 20% full-year net revenue growth in constant dollars, including approximately 0.5 point of contribution from Tink and Currencycloud. The outlook for the second half of the year is positive, with a focus on continuing to invest in new flows and value-added services to drive growth.
