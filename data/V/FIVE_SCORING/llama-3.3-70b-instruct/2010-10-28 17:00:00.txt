Visa's Q4 earnings call revealed strong financial results, with net operating revenues increasing 13% year-over-year to $2.1 billion, driven by double-digit growth in payment volume, cross-border volume, and processed transactions. The company also provided guidance for fiscal 2011, targeting 11-15% net revenue growth and greater than 20% earnings per share growth. Management's tone was optimistic, with a focus on investing in growth initiatives, such as international expansion and innovation, particularly in mobile payments. While there are uncertainties surrounding the Durbin amendment and its potential impact on Visa's business, management expressed confidence in their ability to adapt and navigate the changing regulatory landscape. Overall, the call suggests a positive short-term outlook for the company.
