Visa's Q2 earnings call revealed strong financial performance, with net operating revenue growing 15% year-over-year and net income increasing 23%. The company's international business drove growth, with 62% of revenue growth coming from outside the US. Management expressed confidence in navigating the impact of the Durbin amendment, citing their ability to adapt and compete in a changing regulatory environment. The company also announced a new $1 billion share repurchase program, demonstrating their commitment to returning excess cash to shareholders. Overall, the call suggested a positive outlook for the company's future growth prospects.
