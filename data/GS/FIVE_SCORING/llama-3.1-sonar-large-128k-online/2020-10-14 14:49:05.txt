Goldman Sachs' third quarter 2020 earnings call highlights several positive and stable aspects of the company's performance, which are likely to have a short-term positive impact on the stock price.

### Key Points
- **Record Earnings and Revenue**: The company reported record quarterly earnings of $3.6 billion and $9.68 per share, with net revenues up 30% to $10.8 billion[1].
- **Strong Client Franchise**: Goldman Sachs maintained its leading global position in M&A, equity underwriting, and high yield underwriting, and saw significant market share gains in global markets[1].
- **Robust Performance in Asset Management**: Asset management revenues were up over 70% versus a year ago, driven by higher management and other fees, and gains from equity investments[1].
- **Consumer and Wealth Management Growth**: This segment saw a 13% increase in revenues, driven by higher wealth management assets and consumer banking revenues[1].
- **Capital Position**: The company's Common Equity Tier 1 ratio improved to 14.5%, and they are confident in their capital position and ability to support clients and manage risk[1].
- **Positive Outlook**: Despite the uncertain macroeconomic backdrop, Goldman Sachs is optimistic about future activity, particularly in M&A, and expects continued client engagement and market share gains[1].

### Short-Term Impact
Given these strong results, the company's ability to navigate the challenging COVID-19 environment effectively, and the positive outlook for future activities, the stock is likely to see a short-term positive impact.
