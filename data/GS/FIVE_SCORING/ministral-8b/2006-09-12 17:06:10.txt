The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. reported a strong third quarter 2006, with total net revenues of $7.5 billion, net earnings of $1.7 billion, and EPS of $3.45. Despite a challenging market environment, the firm demonstrated resilience across its businesses. Investment banking revenues were $1.3 billion, down from the second quarter but up 27% year-over-year. Trading and principal investments revenues were $4.7 billion, down 32% from the second quarter but up 7% year-over-year. Asset management and security services also reported strong results. The firm repurchased 3.8 million shares and increased its share repurchase authorization to 73 million shares. The company's management expressed optimism about the medium and long-term prospects, highlighting the diversity of its businesses and the quality of its people.
