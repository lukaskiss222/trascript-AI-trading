### Analysis

The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. reported its second quarter 2012 earnings, with $6.6 billion in net revenues and nearly $1 billion in net earnings. The company highlighted a challenging macroeconomic environment, with significant declines in industry underwriting volumes for debt and equity. Despite these challenges, Goldman Sachs maintained a focus on serving its global client franchise and managing risk prudently. The company also reported a significant increase in its backlog, indicating potential growth in the future.

### Short-Term Implications

1. **Market Uncertainty**: The macroeconomic uncertainty and reduced client activity may continue to impact the company's performance in the near term.
2. **Cost Management**: Goldman Sachs is managing its expenses and capital prudently, aiming to reduce costs by $500 million by the end of the year. This cost-saving effort is likely to continue, potentially impacting the company's short-term profitability.
3. **Share Repurchases**: The company repurchased 14.3 million shares of common stock for a total cost of $1.5 billion, indicating a cautious yet strategic approach to managing its capital.
4. **Regulatory Environment**: The company is preparing for Basel III and other regulatory changes, which may impact its capital requirements and profitability in the near term.

### Conclusion

Given the challenging macroeconomic environment and the company's focus on cost management and risk management, the short-term impact on the stock price is likely to be neutral to slightly negative. The company is well-positioned for long-term growth, but the current uncertainty and regulatory changes may weigh on its stock price in the near term.
