### Analysis

The earnings call for Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. in the third quarter of 2017 highlighted a strong performance across various business units, particularly in investment banking and investment management. Key points include:

1. **Revenue Growth**: Goldman Sachs reported net revenues of $8.3 billion in the third quarter, up 6% sequentially and 2% year-over-year. This growth was driven by strong performance in investment banking and investment management, offsetting weaker FICC performance.

2. **Net Income and EPS**: The company reported net earnings of $2.1 billion and earnings per diluted share of $5.02, with an annualized return on common equity of 10.9%.

3. **Investment Banking**: Investment banking revenues were $1.8 billion, up 4% compared to the second quarter. Advisory revenues were strong, with a 22% increase compared to the second quarter.

4. **Investment Management**: Investment management reported third quarter net revenue of $1.5 billion, with assets under supervision increasing $50 billion sequentially to $1.46 trillion.

5. **Cost Management**: The company maintained a compensation-to-net revenue ratio of 40%, down 100 basis points from the same period last year. This indicates a focus on cost efficiency and operating leverage.

6. **Growth Initiatives**: Goldman Sachs outlined $5 billion in growth initiatives, including opportunities in investment banking, FICC, equities, investment management, and lending. These initiatives are expected to drive an incremental $2.5 billion in annual pretax earnings at a 30% marginal ROE.

7. **Capital Return**: The company repurchased 9.6 million shares of common stock for $2.2 billion in the quarter. The Fed’s non-objection to the capital plan includes an option to raise the dividend by $0.05 per share in the second quarter of next year.

### Conclusion

The earnings call provided a positive outlook for Goldman Sachs, with strong revenue growth and a focus on cost management. The company’s growth initiatives and capital return strategy indicate a commitment to both growth and shareholder value. While there are potential risks and uncertainties, such as regulatory changes and market volatility, the company’s performance and strategic initiatives suggest a positive short-term impact on the stock price.
