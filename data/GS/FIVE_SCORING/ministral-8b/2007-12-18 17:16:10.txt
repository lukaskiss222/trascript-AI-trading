The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. reported a record year with $46 billion in net revenues, $11.6 billion in net earnings, and $24.73 in earnings per diluted share. The company's diverse business mix and global footprint were highlighted, with strong performances across each of its businesses. The Investment Banking division, which posted its second-best quarter with $2 billion in net revenues, had a record year with $7.6 billion in net revenues. The Trading and Principal Investments division also had a record year with $31.2 billion in net revenues, while Asset Management and Securities Services reported strong growth. The company's balance sheet grew, and it repurchased 41.2 million shares for $9 billion. The company's outlook for 2008 is cautious due to the uncertain economic environment, but it remains optimistic about the medium- and long-term outlook.
