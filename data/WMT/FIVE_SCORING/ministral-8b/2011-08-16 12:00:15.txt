### Analysis:
The Walmart earnings call for the second quarter of fiscal year 2012 highlighted strong financial performance despite a challenging global economy. Key highlights include:

- **Financial Results:** Walmart reported diluted earnings per share from continuing operations of $1.09, which was within the guidance of $1.05 to $1.10. Net sales increased 5.5% to $108.6 billion, driven primarily by Walmart International and Sam's Club.
- **Segment Performance:** Walmart U.S. reported flat comp sales without fuel, while Sam's Club delivered a 5% comp increase without fuel. International sales grew 16.2%.
- **Operating Income:** Operating income increased 3.1%, with Walmart U.S. and Sam's Club driving the growth.
- **Free Cash Flow:** Walmart ended the quarter with a positive free cash flow of $4 billion.
- **Shareholder Returns:** The company repurchased $1.4 billion of shares and returned $1.3 billion in dividends during the quarter.

### Conclusion:
The short-term impact on Walmart's stock price is likely to be positive due to the strong financial performance and the company's commitment to cost management and shareholder returns. The increase in EPS guidance for the full year further bolsters investor confidence. The stock is expected to see a positive impact, with a rating of 1.
