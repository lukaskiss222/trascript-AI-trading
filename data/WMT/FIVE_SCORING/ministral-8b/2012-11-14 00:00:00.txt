### Analysis

The earnings call for Walmart Inc. revealed several key points that can impact the stock in the short term. Here are the main factors to consider:

1. **Financial Performance**:
   - **EPS**: The company reported EPS of $1.08, which was within the guidance of $1.04 to $1.09.
   - **Net Sales**: Net sales increased by 3.4% to $113.2 billion, with a constant currency basis showing an increase of 4.9%.
   - **Operating Income**: Operating income grew by 4% to $6.1 billion, outpacing net sales growth.

2. **Segment Performance**:
   - **Walmart U.S.**: Delivered a 1.5% comp sales increase, with strong holiday plans in place.
   - **Sam's Club**: Reported a 2.7% comp sales increase, but operating income growth was challenged by inflation and deflation.
   - **Walmart International**: Achieved solid results despite currency fluctuations, with a 2.4% net sales increase.

3. **Cost Management**:
   - The company is committed to reducing operating expenses as a percentage of sales by at least 100 basis points over the next 5 years.
   - Operating expenses grew by 17 basis points, offsetting a decrease in gross profit rate.

4. **Holiday Season**:
   - Walmart is well-prepared for the holiday season with plans for Black Friday, Thanksgiving, and Cyber Monday events.
   - The company expects strong holiday sales performance.

5. **Global eCommerce**:
   - Walmart is investing in Global eCommerce and leveraging services to drive growth and improved leverage.

### Conclusion

Given the positive financial results and the company’s strong holiday planning, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term. The company’s disciplined approach to cost management and its focus on growth initiatives, such as Global eCommerce, suggest that the stock will continue to perform well despite macroeconomic challenges.
