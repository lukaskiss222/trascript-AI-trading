The earnings call for Walmart's second quarter of fiscal 2013 presents several positive and some cautionary notes that could influence the stock price in the short term.

### Positive Factors:
- **Strong Earnings and Sales Growth**: Walmart reported diluted EPS of $1.18, an 8.3% increase from the previous year, and net sales growth of 4.5% despite currency headwinds.
- **Operational Efficiency**: The company leveraged operating expenses, reduced debt, and improved free cash flow by 50% compared to the previous year.
- **Comp Sales Growth**: Walmart U.S. and Sam's Club delivered positive comp sales, with Walmart U.S. achieving a 2.2% sales comp and Sam's Club seeing a 4.2% comp sales increase without fuel.
- **International Performance**: International segments showed growth, with every market delivering positive comps, and operating income increasing on a constant currency basis.
- **E-commerce Growth**: Walmart saw significant growth in its e-commerce business, which is expected to continue.

### Cautionary Notes:
- **Currency Impact**: The strengthening of the U.S. dollar negatively impacted net sales and operating income, particularly in Mexico and Brazil.
- **FCPA Expenses**: Ongoing expenses related to the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) review will continue through the rest of the year.
- **Economic Uncertainty**: The global economic uncertainty and paycheck cycle pressures could impact consumer spending.

### Short-term Impact:
Given the strong earnings, sales growth, and operational efficiencies, alongside the company's ability to navigate economic uncertainties, the stock is likely to see a positive impact in the short term. However, the ongoing FCPA expenses and currency impacts might slightly temper the enthusiasm.

**Conclusion:**
The overall performance and strategic initiatives outlined in the call suggest a positive short-term impact on the stock price.
