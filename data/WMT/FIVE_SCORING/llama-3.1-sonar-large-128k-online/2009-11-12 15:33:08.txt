2. **Market Share Gains and Price Leadership**: Despite a challenging economic environment, Walmart continued to gain market share across multiple categories. The company's focus on price leadership and customer experience has driven positive traffic and sales, even in the face of deflation in certain categories[4].

3. **Expense Management and Leverage**: Walmart has made significant progress in leveraging its expenses, with SG&A growing less than sales. Corporate overhead expenses were reduced, and Sam’s Club successfully leveraged expenses in October. These efforts are expected to continue, suggesting ongoing operational improvements[4].

4. **International Growth**: The international segment delivered strong performance, with sales growth of 12.1% on a constant currency basis, driven by solid performances in the UK, Mexico, and Brazil. This international growth is a significant contributor to Walmart’s overall performance[4].

5. **Guidance Increase**: The company raised its EPS guidance for the full year, indicating confidence in its ability to maintain strong financial performance through the fourth quarter[4].

Given these factors, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term due to Walmart's strong financial results, effective expense management, and continued market share gains.
