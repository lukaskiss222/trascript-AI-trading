**Analysis:**
McDonald's reported a significant decline in sales due to the COVID-19 pandemic, but the company is taking proactive measures to manage the crisis. Key points include:
- **Sales Impact:** Global comp sales were down 3.4% in Q1 2020, with U.S. comp sales down 25% in April and improving slightly.
- **Reopening Plans:** Reopening efforts are being phased and prioritized based on safety guidelines and market conditions.
- **Franchisee Support:** McDonald's is providing liquidity assistance to franchisees, deferring rent and royalty payments, and working with suppliers and lenders to extend payment terms.
- **Dividend:** The dividend is not under review and remains a priority for the company.
- **CapEx:** CapEx is being reduced by $1 billion, with U.S. experience of the future projects and international openings being delayed.

**Conclusion:**
The short-term outlook for McDonald's stock is likely to be volatile due to the ongoing uncertainty surrounding the pandemic and the company's efforts to manage its franchisees and cash flow. While the company is taking proactive measures to support its franchisees and manage its costs, the stock may face further downward pressure in the near term. However, the company's strong balance sheet and strategic initiatives may provide a solid foundation for long-term growth.
