McDonald's Q1 2008 earnings call highlighted several positive factors that are likely to support the stock in the short term. Here are the key points:

1. **Strong Global Performance**: McDonald's reported a 7.4% increase in comparable sales, with every region contributing to this growth. Europe and Asia-Pacific, Middle East, and Africa (APMEA) had particularly strong performances, with 11.1% and 9.4% comparable sales increases, respectively.

2. **Margin Expansion**: Despite higher commodity costs, the company managed to increase its combined operating margins, driven by strong sales and efficient cost management.

3. **Strategic Initiatives**: The company is executing well on its Plan to Win, including successful menu strategies, reimaging of restaurants, and expansion of convenience initiatives such as extended hours and drive-thrus.

4. **Financial Discipline**: McDonald's demonstrated strong financial discipline with significant share repurchases, dividend payments, and a commitment to returning substantial cash to shareholders.

5. **Positive Outlook**: Management expressed confidence in the company's ability to manage through economic challenges, emphasizing that while the U.S. business is not recession-proof, it is recession-resistant.

Given these factors, the stock is likely to see a positive impact in the short term due to the company's robust global performance, effective cost management, and ongoing strategic initiatives.
