The earnings transcript for McDonald's Corporation indicates several positive trends and strategic initiatives that are likely to have a favorable short-term impact on the stock price.

1. **Positive Comparable Sales**: McDonald's reported a 5.7% increase in U.S. comparable sales for the fourth quarter, driven significantly by the success of all-day breakfast. 2. **Operational Improvements**: The company has made significant operational improvements, including simplified menu boards, increased focus on order accuracy, and the introduction of self-order kiosks and table service in various markets. These changes have improved customer satisfaction and operational efficiency[1].
3. **Value Platform**: McDonald's is testing a value platform, McPick 2, which seems to be resonating with customers, particularly those who are value-conscious. This initiative is expected to continue contributing to sales growth in 2016[1].
4. **Digital Strategy**: The company's mobile app has seen over 7 million downloads, with higher registration and usage rates than industry norms. This digital strategy is expected to drive incremental business and contribute to sales growth in 2016[1].
5. **Financial Performance**: Despite some headwinds, such as labor cost increases and macroeconomic challenges in certain markets, McDonald's reported solid operating income growth and a strong commitment to returning value to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases[1][3].

### Conclusion
Given the positive momentum in comparable sales, operational improvements, successful new initiatives like all-day breakfast and the digital strategy, and a strong financial performance, the stock is likely to see a positive impact in the short term.
