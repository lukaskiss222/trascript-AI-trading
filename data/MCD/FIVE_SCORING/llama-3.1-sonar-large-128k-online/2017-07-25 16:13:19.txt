The earnings transcript for McDonald's Corporation for the second quarter of 2017 indicates several positive trends and initiatives that are likely to have a favorable short-term impact on the stock price.

### Key Points:
- **Strong Global Comparable Sales**: McDonald's reported a 6.6% increase in global comparable sales, driven by guest count growth across all major markets, including the U.S., International Lead Markets, High Growth Markets, and Foundational Markets.
- **Operational Improvements**: The company saw significant operating income growth, benefiting from strong restaurant performance and G&A savings. Earnings per share increased by 21% in constant currencies, excluding strategic charges.
- **Digital and Delivery Initiatives**: McDonald's is making substantial progress with its Velocity Growth Plan accelerators, including mobile order and pay, delivery, and the Experience of the Future (EOTF). These initiatives are showing promising early results, such as higher average checks and strong demand for delivery services.
- **Value Initiatives**: The company has seen success with value promotions, such as the national cold beverage value platform in the U.S., which has driven customer visits and sales.
- **Refranchising and Financial Health**: While refranchising activities are expected to have some near-term dilutive effects on revenue and operating income, they are immediately accretive to free cash flow and are part of a broader strategy to transform McDonald's into a more efficient organization.

### Short-Term Implications:
Given these strong performance metrics, the positive reception of new initiatives, and the company's commitment to remaining competitive on value, it is likely that the stock will experience a positive short-term impact.

### Conclusion:
The combination of strong sales growth, operational improvements, successful new initiatives, and a solid financial outlook suggests that McDonald's stock is poised to perform well in the short term.
