Chevron's Q2 2013 earnings were lower than Q1, primarily due to lower upstream liquids realizations and higher operating expenses, partially offset by a swing in foreign currency effects.  Management highlighted ongoing capital expenditures, particularly in LNG projects, as well as share buybacks, suggesting a commitment to growth despite current market fluctuations. The company expects further working capital release and improved production in the second half of the year; however, the sustained high level of capital expenditures and the fact that upstream earnings continue below expectations could potentially pressure the stock for the foreseeable future
