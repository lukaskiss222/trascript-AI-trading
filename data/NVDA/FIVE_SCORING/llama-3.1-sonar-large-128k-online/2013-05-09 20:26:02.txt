Based on the transcript of NVIDIA's Q1 2014 earnings call, here are the key points that influence the short-term stock price outlook:

## Strong Performance and Guidance
- NVIDIA reported revenue at the upper end of their guidance, driven by strong sales of higher-end GPU products for PC gaming. The company also saw significant growth in their GeForce, Quadro, and Tesla segments[3）。
- The launch of the GeForce GTX Titan and new Quadro K series products has been successful, and the company is optimistic about the future growth of these segments.

## Expanding Markets
- NVIDIA is making significant strides in extending their GPUs beyond the PC market, particularly into the data center with GRID and into new computing devices with Tegra. The GRID VCA and Tegra 4i initiatives show promise and could drive future growth[3].

## Competitive Position and Market Share
- The company is confident about gaining market share, especially with the upcoming Haswell launch. They also highlighted their competitive advantages in the high-end and mainstream processor markets against competitors like Qualcomm and AMD[3].

## Financial Health
- Record gross margins of 54% were achieved, and the company expects to maintain these margins in Q2. The strong financial performance, including EPS above street consensus, adds to the positive outlook[3].

## Short-Term Outlook
- For Q2, NVIDIA anticipates continued strength in consumer gaming, growth in Quadro and Tesla segments, and increasing engagement with GRID enterprise products. The Tegra business is expected to ramp up in the second half of the year, particularly with Tegra 4 and Tegra 4i devices[3].

Given these points, the short-term impact on NVIDIA's stock price is likely to be positive due to the strong earnings report, positive guidance, and promising growth initiatives.
