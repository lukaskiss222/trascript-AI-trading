Based on the transcript of NVIDIA's Q3 fiscal 2014 earnings call, here are the key points that could impact the stock price in the short term:

## Strong Performance in Key Segments
- NVIDIA reported solid financial results driven by strong demand for high-end desktop GPUs, Tegra, Quadro, and Tesla platforms. This includes record revenues for Quadro and Tesla, and significant growth in Tegra sales.

## Growth Drivers
- The company highlighted growth drivers such as PC gaming, professional graphics, compute, and data center segments, which are expected to continue driving revenue.

## Tegra and Automotive
- Tegra revenue more than doubled, with notable success in automotive, which accounts for about 25% of Tegra revenue and is expected to continue growing.

## Product Launches and Innovations
- NVIDIA launched several exciting products, including the GeForce GTX 780 Ti, NVIDIA G-SYNC technology, and GeForce ShadowPlay, which are likely to maintain or increase market share.

## Guidance and Outlook
- The guidance for Q4 indicates flat overall revenue compared to Q3, but with strong sequential growth expected in Tegra. Gross margins are expected to remain stable, and operating expenses are managed well.

## Competitive Position
- Despite competitive pressures, NVIDIA's unique technology offerings, such as its GPU cores and software-defined radios, position it well in the market.

Given these points, the overall tone of the call is positive, highlighting NVIDIA's strong performance across various segments, innovative product launches, and a robust outlook. This suggests that the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term.
