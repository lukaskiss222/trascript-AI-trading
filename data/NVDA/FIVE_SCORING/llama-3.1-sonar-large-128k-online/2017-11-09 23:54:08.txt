NVIDIA's Q3 fiscal 2018 earnings call highlighted several key points that are likely to impact the stock price in the short term:

1. **Record Revenue and Profits**: NVIDIA reported record revenue of $2.64 billion, up 32% year-over-year, and record GAAP EPS of $1.33, up 60% year-over-year. This strong financial performance is typically positively received by the market[2][5].

2. **Growth Across All Platforms**: The company saw significant growth in its gaming, datacenter, professional visualization, and automotive segments. The datacenter segment more than doubled year-over-year, and the gaming segment saw robust demand driven by strong content and e-sports[4].

3. **Volta and AI Momentum**: The adoption of Volta architecture and the company's AI inference solutions are gaining strong traction. Major cloud providers and server OEMs are supporting Volta, indicating a solid future growth trajectory[4].

4. **Positive Outlook**: The guidance for Q4 is strong, with expected revenue of $2.65 billion and continued high gross margins. The company also announced plans to return $1.25 billion to shareholders in fiscal 2019, which is generally viewed positively by investors[4].

5. **Management's Confidence**: Jen-Hsun Huang and Colette Kress expressed confidence in the company's future growth drivers, including AI, autonomous vehicles, and continued dominance in the gaming market. This confidence can boost investor sentiment.

Given these points, the overall sentiment from the earnings call is positive, indicating that the stock is likely to see a short-term rally.
