Boeing's Q2 earnings call revealed strong performance in commercial airplanes, with a 10% increase in revenues and a record backlog of $220 billion. Despite challenges in the defense business, including a charge related to the AEW&C program, the company raised its guidance for 2006 and 2007. Management's tone was positive, with a focus on execution, risk mitigation, and investing in growth programs. The company's commitment to integrity and ethics was also highlighted. Overall, the call suggests a positive short-term outlook for Boeing, driven by strong demand for commercial aircraft and a solid defense portfolio.
