### Analysis:
The Boeing Company's Q1 2006 earnings call revealed strong financial performance, driven by robust commercial airplane deliveries and productivity improvements. The company's outlook for 2006 and 2007 was confirmed, with revenue expected to be approximately $60 billion and $63.5 billion to $64.5 billion, respectively. The EPS guidance was between $3.25 and $3.45 for 2006, and between $4.10 and $4.30 for 2007.

Key points:
1. **Strong Q1 Performance**: Revenue grew 12%, and EPS increased 33%. The Commercial Airplane business delivered 176 orders, including 54 additional firm orders for the 787.
2. **Outlook**: Boeing expects to maintain its strong performance, with a book-to-bill ratio exceeding one during the guidance period.
3. **Challenges**: The company faced challenges related to satellite business, deferred compensation, and R&D expenses. However, management believes these issues will stabilize.
4. **Production**: The 787 program is on track, with the company expecting to begin flight testing in 2008.
5. **Supply Chain**: Boeing is working closely with its supply chain to mitigate risks and ensure timely delivery.
6. **Tax Rate**: The effective tax rate is expected to be close to 31% for 2006, and around 33% for 2007.

### Conclusion:
Given the strong Q1 performance and the company's optimistic outlook for 2006 and 2007, the stock is likely to see a positive impact in the short term. The company's focus on productivity improvements, strong commercial airplane deliveries, and the successful development of the 787 program are likely to drive shareholder confidence and support the stock price.
