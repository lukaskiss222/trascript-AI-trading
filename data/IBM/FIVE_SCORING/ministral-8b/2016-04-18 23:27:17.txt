### Analysis

The earnings call for IBM revealed a mixed bag of results. The company reported strong growth in its strategic imperatives, with revenue growth of 17% on a constant currency basis, and continued investment in new areas such as cloud and cognitive solutions. However, the company also faced challenges, including a decline in the Americas region, particularly in Latin America, and a reduction in revenue from traditional IT services. Additionally, the company reported a pre-tax charge of $1.5 billion due to workforce rebalancing and other transformation costs. Despite these challenges, the company remains optimistic about its future prospects and expects continued growth in its strategic imperatives.

### Conclusion

The short-term impact on IBM's stock price is likely to be positive, given the strong revenue growth in strategic imperatives and the company's continued investment in new areas. However, the stock may face some short-term pressure due to the decline in traditional IT services and the pre-tax charge for workforce rebalancing. Overall, the company's long-term prospects appear strong, and the stock is likely to rally in the coming weeks.
