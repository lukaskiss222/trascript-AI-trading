Kenexa's Q4 2008 earnings call disclosed a challenging economic environment affecting its financial performance, particularly in the RPO segment due to decreased hiring trends and clients moving to minimum commitments. While the ATS revenue remains strong and renewals are high, overall guidance for 2009 suggests cautious optimism with a middle-case scenario projection and significant cost-cutting measures, potentially signaling short-term stability but less immediate positive catalysts. However, the focus on profitability and cash flow management, alongside market share gains in ATS, might mitigate a deep negative impact on stock price in the very short term.[0]
