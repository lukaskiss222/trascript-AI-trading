The earnings transcript for International Business Machines Corporation (IBM) indicates several positive factors that are likely to influence the stock price favorably in the short term:

1. **Strong Financial Performance**: IBM reported a robust fourth quarter with 7% revenue growth, expansion of gross, pre-tax, and net margins, and a 16% increase in earnings per share (EPS) to $4.18. This represents 32 consecutive quarters of EPS growth.

2. **Momentum in Key Segments**: The company saw significant growth in Systems and Technology, particularly in System z mainframes, and strong performance in Software, driven by key areas like Business Analytics and middleware. These segments are expected to continue their momentum into 2011.

3. **Services Backlog and Growth Markets**: IBM's services backlog increased by $4 billion year-to-year, and growth markets showed a 13% revenue growth, indicating strong demand and a positive outlook for these areas.

4. **Cash Flow and Shareholder Returns**: The company generated $8.7 billion in free cash flow for the quarter and $16.3 billion for the full year, and returned $18.6 billion to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases.

5. **2015 Roadmap**: IBM outlined a new roadmap targeting at least $20 of operating EPS by 2015, driven by revenue growth, operating leverage, and share repurchase, which suggests continued long-term growth and financial health.

Given these points, the stock is likely to experience positive short-term impact due to the strong financial performance, ongoing momentum in key segments, and a solid long-term growth strategy.
