Overall positive second quarter results, including record investment banking fees, were overshadowed by high unemployment and the potential long-term impact of upcoming regulatory changes in the card business. The troubles in this segment, along with a mitigated outlook on future reserve requirements, seem to justify the recent compression of the company's stock price. However, JPMorgan's diverse businesses, robust capital position, and market share gains in investment banking provide a strong foundation for the medium-to-long term.
