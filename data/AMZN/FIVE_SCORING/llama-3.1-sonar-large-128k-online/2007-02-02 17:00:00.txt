Amazon's Q4 2006 earnings call presents several key points that indicate a positive short-term outlook for the stock:

1. **Strong Revenue Growth**: Amazon reported a 34% year-over-year increase in worldwide revenue, driven by significant growth in both media and electronics and general merchandise (EGM) categories. This robust growth, especially in EGM, which now accounts for a larger share of sales, is a positive indicator.

2. **Operating Leverage Improvement**: The company achieved significant sequential improvement in operating leverage, with a 24% year-over-year increase in consolidated segment operating income. This indicates better management of costs relative to revenue growth.

3. **Customer and Seller Growth**: Active customer accounts exceeded 64 million, up 16%, and third-party seller participation remained strong, contributing to the company's revenue growth. The adoption of Amazon Prime also continued to be strong, which is seen as beneficial for long-term economics.

4. **Guidance**: The company provided optimistic guidance for Q1 2007 and the full year, expecting continued revenue growth and improvements in operating income and free cash flow. Although there are cautions about uncertainties in exchange rates and global economic conditions, the overall tone is positive.

5. **Strategic Initiatives**: Amazon's investments in new categories, web services, and digital initiatives are expected to drive future growth. While these are not yet at a scale to significantly impact financials, they are seen as promising long-term opportunities.

Given these points, the stock is likely to experience a positive impact in the short term due to the company's strong revenue growth, improved operating leverage, and optimistic guidance.

**Rating: 1**
