Amazon's Q3 2020 earnings call indicates several positive trends that are likely to have a short-term positive impact on the stock price:

- **Strong Financial Performance**: Amazon exceeded analyst expectations in Q3 2020, with significant increases in net sales, operating income, and net income. The company reported EPS of $12.37, far surpassing the expected $7.29, and saw a 37% increase in net sales compared to the same quarter in 2019[1][2/>.
- **Robust Demand and Operational Efficiency**: Despite COVID-19 related costs, Amazon managed to achieve higher-than-expected profitability due to strong demand and operational leverage. The company has invested heavily in expanding its fulfillment and logistics network, which has helped in managing the increased demand efficiently[2].
- **Growth in Key Segments**: AWS and advertising revenues showed strong growth, with AWS continuing to see companies accelerate their plans to move to the cloud. Advertising revenue also saw a significant increase, driven by strong traffic and effective monetization[2].
- **Investments and Future Outlook**: Amazon's investments in capacity expansion, particularly in transportation and fulfillment centers, position the company well for the holiday season and beyond. The company's ability to adapt to the pandemic and its focus on innovation and customer experience are also positive indicators[2].

Given these factors, the stock is likely to see a positive short-term impact.
