Amazon's Q1 2010 earnings call revealed strong revenue growth, with a 46% increase in worldwide revenue to $7.13 billion, and a 62% growth in free cash flow to $2.32 billion. The company's operating income and net income also saw significant increases, with operating income growing 62% to $394 million and net income growing 69% to $299 million. Management's tone was positive, with a focus on driving growth through investments in technology, fulfillment, and marketing. The company's guidance for Q2 was also strong, with expected net sales of between $6.1 and $6.7 billion. Overall, the call suggested that Amazon is well-positioned for continued growth and expansion, driven by its strong e-commerce platform, growing cloud computing business, and increasing investments in new technologies and markets.
