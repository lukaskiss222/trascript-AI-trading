Amazon's Q3 2007 earnings call highlights strong revenue growth (41%) and impressive free cash flow (118% increase), driven by robust sales across e-commerce segments, including Prime membership growth.  Management's tone is optimistic, emphasizing consistent efforts to improve customer experience via pricing, selection, and speed of delivery. While some factors like Harry Potter VII sales contributed positively, the call also acknowledges potential impact on gross margins going forward.  This suggests a short-term positive impact on the stock price, driven by the positive financial results and a reassuring outlook.
