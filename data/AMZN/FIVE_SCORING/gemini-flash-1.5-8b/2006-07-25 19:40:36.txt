Amazon's Q2 2006 earnings call showed continued investment in technology and selection, expanding into new categories like groceries and toys.  While revenue grew strongly, management acknowledged lower-than-expected operating income due to price cuts, investments, and the Toys "R" Us contract termination.  The management's focus on long term growth and customer-centric strategies suggests continued resilience and potential for future profit growth despite near term headwinds.
